Additional Conifers

Thujopsis dolabrata ‘Variegata’

Variegated Elkhorn Cypress

Creamy-white variegation is arranged throughout the thick foliage of this upright-growing tree. Habit can be irregular when young, but becoming more pyramidal with age.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 6-9 Intermediate Sun/Part Shade 5’x2.5′

Sequoiadendron giganteum ‘Pendulum’

Weeping Giant Sequoia

The weeping form of the Giant Redwood has gray-green foliage and incredibly tightly-held pendulous branches that go straight down against the trunk. If the leader stays straight – as it often will – it can be 15′ tall and only 2′ wide. The leader can curve and twist leaving a small footprint, with a spectacular sculptural form. No matter, it makes an incredible tree.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 6-9 Large Sun 10’x1.5′

Pseudotsuga menziesii ‘Mt. Angel Blue’

Mt. Angel Blue Douglas Fir

This compact, blue-green Douglas Fir was found as a witch’s broom at Evergreen Golf Course in Mt. Angel by Stephanie Pratt. It produces cones at its branch tips, making it a very attractive dwarf conifer!

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-8 Dwarf Sun 3’x2′

Cryptomeria japonica ‘Tansu’

Tansu Japanese Cedar

A dense, tight pyramidal conifer that gives the impression of a large tree but on a miniaturized scale. Very dark green foliage gives it a handsome coloration throughout the seasons.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Miniature Sun

Cryptomeria japonica ‘Black Dragon’

Black Dragon Japanese Cedar

Dark green leaves are compressed on the stems of this dense, thick-textured dwarf conifer.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Dwarf Sun/Part Shade 4’x2′
