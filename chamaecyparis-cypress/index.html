Chamaecyparis - Cypress

Chamaecyparis obtusa ‘Nana Lutea’

Filip’s Golden Tears Lawson False Cypress

Gold-highlighted branches hang gracefully from each pendulous branch on this narrow, upright conifer. It was found as a seedling from ‘Dik’s Weeping’ by Edwin Smits.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-8 Dwarf Sun 14’x3′

Cupressus nootkatensis ‘Van Den Akker’

Van den Akker Alaskan Cedar

One of the most narrow conifers in existence. This variety is only about 1′ wide when it is 8′ tall. Sporadic side branches give unique character, or can be removed for a perfectly-narrow vertical element. Green foliage. This species was formerly and is still commonly thought to be in the Chamaecyparis genus. However, it readily hybridizes with other species of Cupressus and bears many similarities, so it is now taxonomically classified as a species of Cupressus. In the past, it was also known as Xanthocyparis nootkatensis and Callitropsis nootkatensis.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 4-8 Intermediate Sun/Part Shade 12’x1.5′

Cupressus nootkatensis ‘Sparkling Arrow’

Sparkling Arrow Alaskan Cedar

Slender weeping branches are dusted with a creamy yellow variegation. The overall effect is like a variegated cascade of delicate foliage. A wonderful narrow accent for a smaller space. This species was formerly and is still commonly thought to be in the Chamaecyparis genus. However, it readily hybridizes with other species of Cupressus and bears many similarities, so it is now taxonomically classified as a species of Cupressus. In the past, it was also known as Xanthocyparis nootkatensis and Callitropsis nootkatensis.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 4-8 Intermediate Sun/Part Shade 12’x3′

Chamaecyparis obtusa ‘Nana Lutea’

Dwarf Yellow Hinoki Cypress

A dwarf yellow conical form with tight clumps of fan-shaped foliage. Will burn in full sun.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Dwarf Part Shade 4’x2′

Chamaecyparis obtusa ‘Nana’

Dwarf Hinoki Cypress

Dark-green foliage is arranged in a beautiful, swirling pattern. This compact, dwarf conifer makes an excellent rock garden plant and is great for bonsai.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Miniature Sun/Part Shade 2’x2′

Chamaecyparis obtusa ‘Melody’

Melody Hinoki Cypress

Lacy, golden-yellow foliage hangs gracefully from each branch of this upright, dwarf conifer. Color changes to a beautiful orange-bronze in winter, while showing no sign of burn in full sun during the summer.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Dwarf Sun/Part Shade 5’x2′

Chamaecyparis obtusa ‘Chirimen’

Chirimen Hinoki Cypress

Juvenile foliage has a very unique appearance on this upright, miniature conifer. Dark green color and a very open habit make this plant a distinctive conifer for the rock garden.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Miniature Sun/Part Shade 1.5×1′

Chamaecyparis lawsoniana ‘Wissel’s Saguaro’

Wissel’s Saguaro Lawson False Cypress

Stout, vertical branches first head out but then head up, giving a branch structure similar to that of a saguaro cactus. The congested, dark-green foliage adds textural interest to this narrow, fairly slow-growing conifer.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-8 Intermediate Sun 6’x2.5′

Chamaecyparis lawsoniana ‘Golden King’

Golden King Lawson False Cypress

Fast growing with golden yellow foliage. Form is rounded when young and pyramidal with age. Foliage has bright golden tips on light-green centers.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-8 Large Sun 12’x 5′

Chamaecyparis lawsoniana ‘Blue Surprise’

Blue Surprise Lawson False Cypress

This narrow, columnar conifer has bright blue, soft-textured foliage. Its slender form and stunning coloration make it a great dwarf for any landscape.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-8 Dwarf Sun 5’x1.5′
