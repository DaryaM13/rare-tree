Japanese Maples

JAPANESE MAPLES

Japanese maples are prized for their incredible assortment of leaf shapes and colors.
The varieties listed below are some of our favorites we regularly produce and have available most years.

Acer sieboldianum ‘Kumoi nishiki’

Kumoi nishiki Siebold Maple

Description:
Each light-green leaf is dusted with a creamy-white variegation, giving this upright tree a mottled appearance. An excellent combination of colors that is truly spectacular.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Intermediate Sun/Part Shade 10 x 5

Acer shirasawanum ‘Sensu’

Sensu Full Moon Maple

Description:
Simply stunning, the fall foliage of this Full Moon maple suggests a flaming sun with golden rays edged in bright scarlet. The pointed leaves emerge light-green with orange-red highlights in spring, and the accents persist in full sun. The name, which means moving fan, hints at the way the deeply divided leaves move in a breeze.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Large Sun/Part Shade 7 x 4

Acer shirasawanum ‘Moonrise’

Moonrise Full Moon Maple

Description:
A fairly new variety that has a coloration similar to ‘Autumn Moon’, bright red growth in spring, more orange-yellow for summer, but can withstand full sun quite well.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Intermediate Sun/Part Shade 7 x 4

Acer shirasawanum ‘Jordan’

Jordan Full Moon Maple

Description:
Spring leaves are bright yellow with pink-orange margins and pink petioles. Summer color is yellow which does not burn in full sun. In shade leaves are yellow-green.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Large Sun/Part Shade 8 x 4

Acer shirasawanum ‘Johin’

Johin Full Moon Maple

Description:
True to its name that means “elegant,” this great new variety of Full Moon maple has burgundy leaves with yellow-green lobe mid-veins that produce a crisp contrast. Think Granny Smith veins with Red Delicious lobes! Beautiful chartreuse and magenta seeds add to the tree’s appeal. Absolutely wonderful!

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Large Sun/Part Shade 9 x 6

Acer shirasawanum ‘Autumn Moon’

Autumn Moon Full Moon Maple

Description:
Vigorous, versatile and unbelievably easy to grow, this small, dramatic Full Moon maple boasts orange-red highlights on yellow leaves. The unusual coloring, strongest in full sun, lasts from when leaves emerge in spring until they go rich orange-red in fall. In shade, leaves start out with orange-red highlights, and settle on yellow in early summer.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Intermediate Sun/Part Shade 7 x 4

Acer shirasawanum ‘Aureum’

Golden Full Moon Maple

Description:
The foliage of this prized Full Moon maple virtually glows throughout the season. Especially in a site with filted light, the fan-shaped leaves emerge bright yellow in spring, then gradually soften to yellow-green, a color that holds all summer. In fall, bright yellow, orange and scarlet, might all appear on a single leaf. Colors are more subdued in a shady spot. Attractive branching is striking in winter.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Intermediate Part Shade 6 x 4

Acer pictum ssp. mono ‘Usugumo’

Fleecy Cloud Painted Maple

Description:
Thin, lightly-colored leaves on this fascinating maple are distinctively beautiful. The leaf surface appears to be stretched across the ribs of the leaf–a bat-wing maple. Like us, you’ve probably wanted one of these since you saw the awesome photos in Vertrees! An awesome variety with variegated bat-wing curled leaves. Incredibly difficult to propagate, therefore seldom seen.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 6-9 Dwarf Sun/Part Shade 8 x 5

Acer pictum ssp. mono ‘Hoshi yadori’

Variegated Painted Maple

Description:
Broad leaves on this upright maple are splashed with a gorgeous creamy-yellow variegation. A rare maple that really makes a statement in the landscape!

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 6-9 Intermediate Sun/Part Shade 7 x 5.5

Acer palmatum ‘Yama nishiki’

Snow Peak Japanese Maple

Description:
An upright form with whitish variegation on leaf margins. The variegation is quite distinct from other varieties, giving this new cultivar an impressive appearance.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Dwarf Sun/Part Shade 2 x 4

Acer palmatum ‘Yama hime’

Dwarf Mountain Japanese Maple

Description:
A dwarf form with a bun or dome shape and light green leaves. The name means “dwarf mountain.” New leaves are yellow-green and fall colors are orange and yellow.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Dwarf Sun/Part Shade 2 x 4

Acer palmatum ‘Ukigumo’

Ukigumo or Floating clouds Japanese maple

Description:
Variegation in pastel tones and leaves that may curl or twist distinguish this attractive Japanese maple. In spring, the light green leaves display white-pink spots, sometimes covering large areas. In summer, the variegation softens, and in fall, the foliage goes gold. The shrub-like tree benefits from protection from afternoon sun.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Intermediate Part Shade/Shade 8 x 4

Acer palmatum ‘Twombly’s Red Sentinel’

Twombly’s Red Sentinel Japanese Maple

Description:
Perfect for a smaller garden, this outstanding purple-red variety forms a column that gets about 10′ tall but only 3′ wide in 10 years. Found as a witches broom, it develops intense color that lasts through the growing season and then flashes into spectacular red for fall. Although it has proven somewhat difficult to propagate, the narrow selection grows nicely.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 6-9 Intermediate Sun/Part Shade 8 x 2.5

Acer palmatum ‘Tsuma gaki’

Tsuma gaki Japanese Maple

Description:
Yellow-green leaves emerge cupped with burgundy-orange tips. It looks as if the entire tree is unfolding soft flowers (the leaves) – others have described it as flocking birds. Tip color lasts until early summer. Summer leaves are flat and held horizontally. Fall color is reds and oranges.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Dwarf Sun/Part Shade 8 x 4

Acer palmatum ‘Tsukasa Silhouette’

Shining Silhouette Japanese Maple

Light-green foliage emerges a yellow-green with pink-tipped leaf edges. A beautiful, bushy and narrow tree with small leaves.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Large Sun/Part Shade 9 x 4

Acer palmatum ‘Tiger Rose’

Tiger Rose Japanese Maple

Description:
This medium sized upright has relaxed branches giving a pendulous feel similar to ‘Omure yama.’ Spring leaves are bright pink, becoming creamy white with green veins and orange-pink margins. Summer foliage is light green with darker green venation. Fall color is red. Color is much more intense in partial shade.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Intermediate Part Shade 8 x 5

Acer palmatum ‘Taylor’

Taylor Japanese Maple

Description:
A dwarf deciduous shrub with a bushy upright form. Thin willowy branches feature small green leaves edged in pink. Very colorful throughout the year.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 6-9 Dwarf Sun/Part Shade 6 x 3

Acer palmatum ‘Spring Delight’

Spring Delight Japanese Maple

Description:
Named for its tasty spring show, this vigorous laceleaf Japanese maple boasts yellow-green spring leaves that look as if they have been dipped in cranberry juice. Similar to Viridis in habit, this chance seedling changes to bright orange in fall. Dissectum.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Intermediate Part Shade 3 x 6

Acer palmatum ‘Shigitatsu sawa’

Shigitatsu sawa Japanese Maple

Description:
Contrasting colors distinguish this unique Japanese maple. Creamy white spring leaves are decorated with prominent green veins; as they turn green in summer, the veins darken too. Orange and red complete the two-tone show in autumn. Protection from full sun, which can burn the leaves, benefits the beautiful, medium size tree.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Intermediate Part Shade 8 x 5

Acer palmatum ‘Shidava Gold’

Shidava Gold Japanese Maple

Description:
This dwarf Japanese maple, discovered as a witches broom on Aoyagi, offers dramatic four-season interest with flashy foliage and striking bark. New spring leaves emerge yellow-green, turn bright green during summer and golden yellow for fall. In winter, the compact tree’s intensely green bark brightens otherwise gray days. Great for container or front of border, it rarely reaches six feet.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Large Sun/Part Shade 9 x 4

Acer palmatum ‘Sango kaku’

Coral Bark or Coral Tower Japanese Maple

Description:
Highly regarded for its outstanding, almost fluorescent coral bark, this upright, spreading Japanese maple produces a fabulous show all four seasons. Red margins decorate the yellow-green spring leaves that gradually change to bright green in summer. Yellow-gold and apricot shades produce a striking fall show. The bark, with colors that intensify as winter approaches, brightens up gray days and glows against snow. Youngest wood bears the strongest color. ‘Sango kaku’ means “coral tower.”

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Intermediate Sun/Part Shade 7 x 5

Acer palmatum ‘Mikawa yatsubusa’

Mikawa yatsubusa Japanese Maple

Description:
A truly unique dwarf Japanese maple, this cultivar produces layers of small leaves that overlap each other because of close leaf spacing and short petioles. The effect is striking: a very dense, compact plant with a diverse range of leaf size and color. New growth seems to include the whole color spectrum between yellow and green, with older growth turning dark glossy green. In fall, a different color range takes over, spanning the range between yellow and scarlet. Excellent for small landscapes.
