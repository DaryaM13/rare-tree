Larix - Larch

Larix marschlinsii (x) ‘Lilian’

Lilian Dunkeld Larch

Found as a witch’s broom in The Netherlands; a compact larch with short foliage and a fairly dense form.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 4-8 Dwarf Sun 3’x3′

Larix laricina ‘Michigan Tower’

Michigan Tower American Larch

A fast-growing, fairly narrow selection found by Josh Horky and collected with Josef Braeu of Edelweiss Nursery. The blue-green colour and the narrow form make this deciduous conifer a very exciting selection.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 2-8 Large Sun

Larix laricina ‘Big Mama’

Paper Lanterns Japanese Larch

This irregular, compact dwarf has bright, bluish-green needles that turn golden-yellow in autumn.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 2-8 Dwarf Sun

Larix kaempferi ‘Paper Lanterns’

Paper Lanterns Japanese Larch

Dainty, yellow-green cones are perched amidst the fresh green tufts of needles on the ascending branches of this narrow, vigorously-upright larch before turning to brown, giving the appearance of paper lanterns hanging from the bare branches in winter.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 4-8 Large Sun 8’x3′

Larix gmelinii ‘Miss Vicky’

Miss Vicky Dahurian Larch

This slow-growing larch is a very unique find! Originating as a witch’s broom discovered by Dennis Hermsen at an arboretum in Madison, Wisconsin and named for his youngest daughter. This Siberian species is hardy to Zone 1! It is the first larch to emerge in the spring, and the last one to lose its needles in the autumn. A true collector’s find!

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 1-7 Dwarf Sun/Part Shade 2’x2′

Larix decidua ‘Puli’

Puli European Larch

Deciduous. This heavily weeping selection has lime green foliage in spring. Fall color is golden to yellow. Excellent waterfall form if staked, otherwise an equally interesting mound form.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 2-8 Intermediate Sun 4’x2′

Larix decidua ‘Krejči’

Krejči European Larch

This fascinating dwarf larch has tufts of twisted needles spaced irregularly on the twisting, open branches. Grows to be an irregular dwarf. It originated as a root sprout in the Czech Republic in 1984.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 2-8 Intermediate Sun 4’x3′
