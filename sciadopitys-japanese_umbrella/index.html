Sciadopitys - Japanese Umbrella Pine

Sciadopitys verticillata ‘Picola’

Picola Japanese Umbrella Pine

This dense, squat, pyramidal conifer has rich, dark-green foliage year-round. It is one of the slowest-growing varieties of Japanese umbrella pine.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 4-9 Dwarf Sun/Part Shade 3.5’x3′

Sciadopitys verticillata ‘Joe Kozey’

Joe Kozey Japanese Umbrella Pine

A columnar variety of the Japanese Umbrella Pine. The tight upright branches are increasingly capable of withstanding snow load compared to the species and other cultivars. 

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 4-9 Large Sun/Part Shade 8’x2.5′

Sciadopitys verticillata ‘Grüne Kugel’

Green Ball Japanese Umbrella Pine

This slow-growing form of Japanese Umbrella Pine will develop into a rounded, dwarf, but as it ages, it will become more pyramidal. Wonderful, thick, dark-green foliage provides a handsome and stout appearance. 

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 4-9 Dwarf Sun/Part Shade 2’x1′

Sciadopitys verticillata ‘Explosion’

Explosion Japanese Umbrella Pine

This rare Sciadopitys has remarkably thin needles that are exceptionally long. Perhaps the most distinctive of the umbrella pines. Peculiar in appearance but super cool! 

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 4-9 Intermediate Sun/Part Shade 5’x2′
