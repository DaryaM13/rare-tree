Taxus - Yew

Taxus cuspidata ‘Nana Aurescens’

Dwarf Golden Japanese Yew

A low-growing, mounding yew with brilliant golden-yellow color that acquires an orange hue in winter.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 4-9 Dwarf Part Shade

Taxus baccata ‘White Pillar’

White Pillar English Yew

This narrow yew has dark-green foliage with striking white-colored margins! The color doesn’t fade in winter, making this an exceptional selection; colorful and narrow.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 6-9 Dwarf Sun/Part Shade 5’x1.5′

Taxus baccata ‘Watnong Gold’

Watnong Gold English Yew

Bright gold colour is most noticeable on the newer growth with a vibrant yellow persisting on the leaf margins throughout the rest of the year. Will form a spreading, somewhat bushy form with time.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 6-9 Dwarf Sun/Part Shade 3’x4′

Taxus baccata ‘Standishii’

Golden English Yew

Bright golden foliage is most noticeable in areas where the plant was exposed to the sun. Forms a narrow pillar with age.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 6-9 Intermediate Sun/Part Shade 5’x2′

Taxus baccata ‘Silver Spire’

Silver Spire English Yew

As its name suggests, this upright cultivar of English Yew has a silver-colored variegation on the margins of the leaves. This bright variegation is accentuated in the winter months. Its upright form and color combination make it a nice landscape plant.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 6-9 Intermediate Sun/Part Shade 5’x1.5′
