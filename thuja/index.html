Thuja - Arborvitae

Thuja occidentalis ‘Smaragd’

Emerald Green Arborvitae

Perhaps the most common conifer, being used as the most popular hedge plant in America. Its dense, columnar habit lends it well to this application.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 4-8 Dwarf Sun/Part Shade

Thuja plicata ‘Whipcord’

Whipcord Western Red Cedar

Thin, wiry branches protrude in every direction from this dwarf bun of peculiar foliage. Discovered by Barbara Hupp.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-8 Dwarf Sun/Part Shade/Shade 2.5’x2.5′

Thuja orientalis ‘Franky Boy’

Franky Boy Oriental Arborvitae

A phenomenal new introduction with stringy light green and golden foliage that brightens to orange-butterscotch in the winter. New growth cascades over old, creating a fountain of foliage strings. Makes a fantastic small shrub which can be aggressively pruned.

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 5-9 Dwarf Sun/Part Shade/Shade 4’x3′

Thuja occidentalis ‘Malonyana Holub’

Malonyana Holub Arborvitae

Thick, dark-green foliage develops on tall, columnar stems. This unique branching structure gives a fascinating structure to this conifer–sculptural and striking!

Hardiness Growth Rate Esposure HxW@10yrs
Zones 4-8 Dwarf Sun/Part Shade 3.5’x2′
